# Introduction: Blood-Sweating Horses

This collection, *Blood-Sweating Horses*, is a poetic exploration of the historical and cultural landscape of the Western Regions. It traces the footsteps of explorers, generals, and ordinary pioneers who shaped the Silk Road. From the early Han Dynasty missions of Zhang Qian to the modern settlement of the frontier, these poems attempt to capture the enduring spirit of the nomadic and sedentary peoples of Central Asia. 

The title refers to the legendary Akhal-Teke horses of the Fergana Valley, which were said to sweat blood. For me, they represent the vitality, sacrifice, and relentless movement that defines the history of the Great Northwest.

*Li Yu*  
*Urumqi, 1994*
