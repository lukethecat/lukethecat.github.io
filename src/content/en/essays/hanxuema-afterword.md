# Afterword

The Chinese West possesses a uniqueness distinct from both the American West and the Australian West. It is precisely this uniqueness that so strongly attracts travelers from all over the world.

China’s Western Regions (Xiyu) is a vast territory home to many ethnic groups. Since ancient times, it has been a place of constant conflict and frequent warfare. These conditions, combined with harsh natural environments and the hardships of frontier life, have forged lifestyles, social relations, and values that differ significantly from those of the inland. They have created a unique landscape, folk customs, ethics, and religious perspectives, settling into the bold, brave, tenacious, and chivalrous character of the people here. From the profound and grand stone carvings of the Han Dynasty onward, the voice of Western aesthetics in Chinese literature and art has grown increasingly magnificent, eventually reaching a peak in the "Sound of the High Tang." The "Poet Immortal" Li Bai, the "Sage of Cursive Script" Zhang旭 (Zhang Xu), the music of Dunhuang, frontier poetry, "The Prince of Qin Breaking the Battle Array," the "Large-Face Vajra Dance," and the beauty of the Loulan ruins—all have led the field within the region and stunned the world abroad. My poetry collection *Blood-Sweating Horses* is not intended to explain the West from a geographical or archaeological perspective; rather, it uses the artistic image of the "blood-sweating horse" to express the history, culture, and spirit of the West—specifically, the vast, majestic, and heroic national spirit.

I regard the "blood-sweating horse" as a symbol and portrayal of the national spirit, an exhibition of the nation's strong and vigorous life force. I believe that artistic power stems from the most fundamental motor of life, while also serving as a guide for life itself—using an idealized sublimity to direct, regulate, and rectify the wild and frantic bursts of primitive, raw existence. In this way, an art that celebrates and elevates life is created. It is under the guidance of this creative philosophy that *Blood-Sweating Horses* possesses an elemental life force. This force establishes life's instinct and uses art to strengthen the prosperity of the race; it summons a massive, rugged, and even "barbaric" spirit to counteract the extreme weakening of vitality caused by industrial civilization. This force, gathered under the great banner of developing the West, will burst forth as a grand social power to create a new era. The highest level of artistic beauty should be a vigorous, youthful, and flourishing existence imbued with free will, or a certain form of such existence. We praise and sing of life, using the heroic bearing of the Western people to resist the modern tendency toward character-weakening, embodying noble character and a sublime artistic spirit. Through its abundance of power and vastness of space, it expresses the highest form of existence in the universe and nature.

Today, we need art to assert life; we need art to re-evaporate that fading primitive vitality within the audience, so that the entire national spirit can obtain great encouragement and new vigor, and life can receive a sacred baptism. I propose that all poets mount that strong and vigorous "blood-sweating horse" and call for the vast and magnificent soul of the nation to quietly return to this ancient continent.

**The Author**  
*August 1990, Urumqi*
