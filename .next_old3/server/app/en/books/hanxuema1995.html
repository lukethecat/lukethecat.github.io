Li Yu / 1995

O, the New Continent of Central Asia

That Glorious Arrow Must Still Be in Flight / Cast toward the Distant West

The blood-red setting sun is about to die

The dark-gold curtain of night about to fall

Zhang Qian1 drives his herded flock homeward

A desolate pastoral song blends with the mournful reed-pipe

Piercing the haze of an autumn twilight

From a distant felt tent a thread of smoke rises

Like a dark-gold peony from his native Hanzhong

Slowly blooming across the sky of the Western Regions

He turns to gaze at that dark-gold cloud far to the west

The flock surges like waves beneath his horse

O, glorious setting sun

Already it has tumbled

Beyond the dark-gold horizon

Yet that glorious arrow must still be in flight

Cast toward the distant west

Cast toward the distant road ahead

Cast toward the vast plains

Of the Great Yuezhi3, longed for day and night

注释

  1. 1.Zhang Qian—Zhang Qian (c. 164–114 BC) — Han Dynasty diplomat and explorer who pioneered the Silk Road route to Central Asia on behalf of Emperor Wu.
  2. 2.Hujia—Hujia — an ancient reed instrument of northern nomadic peoples, known for its desolate, tragic timbre, often used as a trope in frontier poetry.
  3. 3.Great Yuezhi—Great Yuezhi — a nomadic people originally from the Hexi Corridor, later migrated to Central Asia. They were Zhang Qian's destination in seeking an alliance against the Xiongnu.
  4. 4.Han Staff—Han Staff (jié) — the imperial envoy's staff of authority, a bamboo rod topped with yak-tail tassels symbolizing the emperor's mandate and mission.

The Clasped Hands Slowly Loosened / The Tear-Filled Eyes Abruptly Turned Away

The boundless steppe has fallen asleep

Snow-crowned Qilian has fallen asleep

The child in the felt tent must have fallen asleep too

Standing before the black steed

Gazing in silence

At his weeping wife of the Western Regions

Zhang Qian also weeps

Tear-filled eyes meet tear-filled eyes

Hands clasp hands tightly

Her curling, tawny hair drifts in the breeze

In the pale moonlight

In the pale starlight

Her eyes, blue as Lake Qinghai, gleam with tears

Pale as the moonlight

Pale as the starlight

Her tears, ripe as grapes, fall

Reflecting the pale moonlight

Reflecting the pale starlight

The black steed stamps the grass without ceasing

Its sound oppressive

A heavy sheepskin water-bag

And a tall stack of dried naan still hang from the saddle

Zhang Qian gazes in silence at

His weeping wife

Gazes in silence at

The wife of the Western Regions with whom he has lived for years

The clasped hands slowly loosen

The tear-filled eyes abruptly turn away

注释

  1. 1.Qilian Mountains—Qilian Mountains — located at the border of Gansu and Qinghai, meaning 'Heavenly Mountain' in Xiongnu. It is the southern barrier of the Hexi Corridor and an ancient crucial passage to the Western Regions.

O Great Yuezhi / The Holy Land He Has Dreamed of for Eleven Springs

How long he has been riding, he does not know

Whether he is still riding, he is not sure

In the darkness, not even the dim horizon is visible

Nothing is visible

All seems frozen in the profound dark

Still he presses on with all his strength

The distance between him and Tangyi Fu1 slowly narrows

But where is the mysterious Great Yuezhi?

Where is the distant Great Yuezhi?

Just follow loyal, capable Tangyi Fu1

Just ride into the pitch-black west

One day they shall reach their destination

O Great Yuezhi

The holy land he has dreamed of for eleven springs

Through more than four thousand unbearable days in foreign lands

The name he murmured ceaselessly

Through more than four thousand unbearable nights in foreign lands

The name he murmured ceaselessly

From the galloping saddle he turns once more

To gaze at a silhouette already lost from sight

注释

  1. 1.Tangyi Fu—Tangyi Fu — Zhang Qian's loyal Xiongnu companion and guide throughout his perilous journey to the Western Regions.

Bathed in the Cool Radiance / That Daylight Had Yearned for Like Shade

That tyrannous sun —

When did it sheathe its scorching golden arrows?

A crescent moon above the distant western Tianshan

Smiles at him tenderly

Waking from a burning, dreadful dream

He bathes in the cool, liquid light

Bathes in the cool radiance

That daylight had yearned for like shade

He clenches his fist ever tighter

The icy grains of sand

Trickle slowly through his fingers to the ground

He can hear the soft rustling sound

As though the mountain stream of home flows again before him

Moistening his gaunt cheeks

Moistening his cracked lips

The Pioneer of the Silk Road Smiled from Horseback / Gazing Westward

No longer narrow as the Hexi Corridor1

No longer a winding trail

Hemmed between chains of mountains

Gazing into the distance

Slowly riding west

As though on a ship of the Gobi

As though listening to the distant melody of camel bells

Nothing obstructs the view

O, the new continent of Central Asia

Mysterious and vast

Has miraculously

Appeared before the explorer bearing the Han envoy's staff

Zhang Qian smiled

The pioneer of the Silk Road2 smiled from horseback

Gazing westward

Gazing toward the still-unseen

Direction of the Great Yuezhi

From his cracked lips, crimson drops of blood trickled down

  2. 2.Silk Road—Silk Road — the ancient network of trade routes connecting China to Central and West Asia, and Europe, named for the Chinese silk that was heavily traded westward.

Beneath Qilian, Silence Has Fallen

He Kept His Eyes Fixed / On the Distant Icy Peaks of Qilian

The iron torrent flows slowly

The great desert flows slowly

Gazing from horseback

He can no longer bear to see

The ash-brown flickering like flame

He keeps his eyes fixed

On the distant icy peaks of Qilian

So crystalline and cool

They chill to the marrow

Floating on the surging, ash-brown clouds

Now and then flashing a blue, spectral light

Like a verdant mirage

Shimmering always on the horizon

Huo Qubing1 licked his cracked lips

Licked the beading blood

注释

  1. 1.Huo Qubing—Huo Qubing (140–117 BC) — brilliant young Han general who defeated the Xiongnu at Qilian, securing the Hexi Corridor. He died prematurely at age 24.

Is That the Long-Dreamed Crescent Moon Lake? / The Crescent Lake That Once Graced His Dreams?

Still galloping

Into the deep night

Only the drumming of hooves

Mingling with the wind's drizzle

Dunhuang1 in March

The wind is still biting

Iron-grey thickets, not yet budding with elm coins,

Steeped in darkness, sweep past

Wave-like iron-grey dunes sweep past

Their outlines so faint

The night sky, too, is iron-grey

Wild pigeons startle into flight

Those startled pigeons are iron-grey too

The beating of their wings fades in an instant

Ahead, a crescent moon rises

In the iron-grey night sky

Glimmering with a pale, spectral light

Is that the Crescent Moon Lake he has long dreamed of?

The crescent lake that once graced his dreams?

注释

  1. 1.Dunhuang—Dunhuang — an ancient oasis city at the westernmost end of the Hexi Corridor, a hub of the ancient Silk Road, world-renowned for its Mogao Caves.
  2. 2.Crescent Lake—Crescent Lake (Yueyaquan) — located at the foot of the Mingsha Mountains in Dunhuang, named for its crescent moon shape, historically praised as 'the first spring in the desert'.

The Carrier Pigeon Circled Hurriedly for a Moment / Then Flew Eastward

The soldier had just fallen

When he hastily tore open his black tunic

A carrier pigeon rose from his breast

His hot blood

Had already stained the white plumage crimson

Still glistening wet

Skimming past the steeds that galloped by the soldier

Past the sand-grains lifted like mist

Past the overcast sky, dull as sand

Soaring upward

Lying face-up on the embattled earth

Iron cavalry thundering across his body

Like storm clouds sweeping before his eyes

His clenched hands go slack

Yet his eyes still gaze at the vast heavens

The carrier pigeon circled hurriedly for a moment

Then flew eastward

Across the reflection in his pupils — of flashing blades and gleaming swords —

It cast a dark-brown shadow

Beneath Qilian, silence has fallen

His phantom-like black steed

Stands frozen as a statue beneath Qilian

Beneath Qilian, all is silent

He still gazes at the icy peak before him

So crystalline and cool

As though that peak were his eternal monument

He still gazes at the grasslands before him

Boundless as the sea

As though those grasslands were his eternal bed

The wind whispers against his crimson cloak

Like the rustling of silk banners

Like the murmur of his mother's lullaby

He was only twenty-four when he

Let the reins slip from his fingers

Only twenty-four

When he closed his eyes for the last time

But the war drum in his breast still thunders

Thunder upon thunder

Resounding through the Hexi Corridor

Through the vast deserts and grasslands of the Western Regions

His phantom-like black steed

Still stands beneath Qilian

Like a sculpture

Motionless as a mountain

The earth is silent

Beneath Qilian, silence has fallen

Standing in the Wine-Sweet, Dark-Gold Sunset / Standing in the Future Bazaars of the Silk Road

The setting sun paused a moment before slipping away

Still gilding the young general's bronze cheeks

The wind-worn, time-struck features of his face

Still bore the flush of that last battle

His war horse bent down to drink from the spring

Ripples spread across the water

Like the silk road unfurling toward the west

Standing in the wine-sweet, dark-gold sunset

He could smell the fragrance drifting from the future

The mingled scent of grapes and pomegranates

From bazaars not yet built

Standing in the future bazaars of the Silk Road

Standing in streets paved with moonlight and camel bells

He raised the bronze goblet

Let the wine of Jiuquan1 pour like gold

Into the spring that the emperor had named for him

For his soldiers to share, one sip at a time

The spring flows on

Even now, still fragrant

At the foot of Qilian

In the wine-sweet sunset

注释

  1. 1.Jiuquan—Jiuquan ('Wine Spring') — a city in Gansu named after a legend that General Huo Qubing poured imperial wine into a spring to share it with all his troops.

Wild Poppies

Beneath the Galloping Hooves / Waves of Wild Poppies Unfurl

She rides from the deep grassland

From blooming wild poppies, jade-green and crimson

Her braids streaming behind her

Like two young swallows in flight

Beneath the galloping hooves

Waves of wild poppies unfurl

Crimson and boundless as the sunset

Stretching in every direction

The young Wusun3 princess

Daughter of the grassland

Galloping toward the setting sun

With the wind in her face

How far away the Central Plains must be

Yet flowers of the Central Plains bloom here too

There are no walls on this steppe

Only wild poppies

Blooming ceaselessly

And the Ili River, winding southward

Carrying crimson petals

Through the valley

Into the distance

注释

  1. 1.Künes—Künes — an alpine meadow in the middle section of the Tianshan Mountains (Xinyuan County, Ili, Xinjiang), rich in water and grass, the core pasture of the ancient Wusun State.
  2. 2.Princess Xijun—Liu Xijun — daughter of Liu Jian, King of Jiangdu in the Western Han. In 105 BC, Emperor Wu sent her to marry the Wusun King (Kunmo) in a political alliance.
  3. 3.Wusun—Wusun — an ancient nomadic kingdom located in the Ili River valley (modern Kazakhstan/Xinjiang) that allied with the Han Dynasty through marriage diplomacy.

The July Night Is / So Hushed and Still

The July night is

So hushed and still

Even the Ili River1 has quieted its murmur

On the moonlit steppe

Wild poppies glow

Their crimson petals glistening with dew

Like tears she has not yet shed

She stands beneath the Tianshan moon

This girl sent from Chang'an

Homesick for the south

But the grassland wind

Already smells of home — of earth

She cannot tell whether it is the steppe or her heart that sighs

注释

  1. 1.Ili River—Ili River — major river flowing from the Tianshan Mountains into Lake Balkhash, historically the cradle of Wusun and Kazakh cultures.

A Dark-Brown Wild Poppy Has Withered / Yet Its Petals Still Release a Heady Fragrance

A dark-brown wild poppy has withered

Yet its petals still release a heady fragrance

Time has turned the crimson to brown

Yet cannot erase the perfume

She pressed the petals between the pages

Of a letter she would send to Chang'an

But there was no courier to the east

No wild goose that far

So the fragrance stayed

Stayed in the valley of the Ili

Stayed in the windswept steppe

Even now, two thousand years later

Before Her, the Ili River / Has No Trace of That Crimson Leaf That Carried Her Childhood Heart

On the day she left Chang'an

A child pressed a red leaf into her hand

She placed it on the water of the Wei River

And watched it drift westward

Through the Hexi Corridor

Through the Jade Gate Pass

Across deserts she had never imagined

The leaf in her memory kept drifting

She never saw that leaf again —

That red leaf carrying her childhood heart

Before her, the Ili River

Has no trace of that crimson leaf

Only the fallen petals of wild poppies

Though they too are red
